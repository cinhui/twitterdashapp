Visualizing the Elite





Motivation



It's been an on-going phenomena (Thanks to Sammy Guervava) that everytime Chris Jericho makes his entrance into the ring and the song "Judas" plays, the entire arena would sing along. However, now that AEW Dynamite is being aired in a closed location with no audience, we miss that crowd affect.
I saw on Reddit and Twitter that fans wanted to virtually recreate that phenomena, by tweeting videos of them singing Judas during Dynamite, using the hashtags #JudasChallenge and #WorldWideCrowd.

As part of my AEW Dynamite Twitter Activity analysis, I wanted to capture the affects of this #JudasChallenge. Here's a summary of the findings.

Twitter fans sing Judas as Chris Jericho enters the Ring



The tweets were collected on Mar 25, 2020 from 05:59PM CST to 10:19PM CST, for approximately 4 hours and 19 mins. This line graph shows the number of tweets that mention either #JudasChallenge or #WorldWideCrowd over the span of time. The vertical black dotted lines are reference lines for when the show started 07:00PM CST (or 19:00) and when it concluded 09:00PM CST (or 21:00). The vertical green dotted line (08:45PM CST or 20:48) is when @AEWonTNT sent a tweet encouraging the fans on Twitter to sing along as Chris Jericho is about to make his entrance.



As shown in the graph, Twitter fans were tweeting and retweeting renditions of Judas throughout the evening. The blue line corresponds to the total number of tweets that mentioned either hashtags. The red line corresponds to tweets that were not retweets. In other words, they contained original content in their tweets. there's a large spike in Twitter activity at 8:48pm CST, just minutes after AEW's tweet. The #JudasChallenge worked and fans continued to sing for hours after wards.
